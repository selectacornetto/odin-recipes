SOPAS

Sopas, the Filipino version of a creamy macaroni soup, is a dish that highlights the country’s ability to adapt foreign influences into something uniquely local.

Its origins can be traced to the American colonial period in the early 20th century, when pasta, canned milk, and processed meats became more accessible in the Philippines. Over time, Filipinos transformed these ingredients into a warm, filling soup that is now considered a staple of home cooking.

Beyond its history, sopas carries strong cultural associations with comfort and care. It is commonly served to children, the elderly, or anyone feeling unwell, making it a dish tied to nourishment and recovery. Families also prepare it during rainy days, school breaks, or simple gatherings, giving it a sense of familiarity and warmth in everyday life.

While the basic elements remain constant—macaroni, milk-based broth, and meat or chicken—sopas has many variations that reflect household creativity and resourcefulness. Some families add vegetables for balance, while others emphasize richer broths for heartier meals. Despite these differences, the dish consistently embodies hospitality and the Filipino value of looking after loved ones.

Note: This recipe feeds 2. You may also omit the chicken and fish sauce to make it vegetarian, but adjust the cooking time accordingly.

INGREDIENTS

  • 250g elbow macaroni
  • 250g chicken breast, boneless and cubed
  • 2L water
  • 500mL milk
  • 20mL vegetable oil
  • 10mL fish sauce
  • 1 large cabbage, chopped
  • 1 large carrot, cubed
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 large garlic, minced
  • Fine salt
  • Black pepper crushed

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a pot, sauté garlic and onion in oil until browned.
  2. Add the chicken and sauté until the outside is cooked.
  3. Fill the pot with water and bring to a boil.
  4. Throw in the macaroni and cook until halfway done.
  5. Add the milk, cabbage, carrot, and fish sauce.
  6. Once the macaroni is done, add salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot.
